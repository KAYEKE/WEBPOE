Our Projects & Campaigns

Making a measurable difference through targeted environmental action

Urban Tree Drive

Our flagship tree-planting program bringing green spaces to urban Cape Town.

Every first Saturday of the month, we organize community tree-planting events across Cape Town. We work with schools, parks, and community centers to identify areas that need greening and involve local residents in the planting process.

Impact:

  • 500+ trees planted since 2025
  • 12 urban locations transformed
  • 200+ volunteers per event
  • 85% tree survival rate

Zero-Waste Challenge

Transforming waste habits through education and practical workshops.

Cape Town produces millions of tons of waste annually, much of it preventable. Our Zero-Waste Challenge helps individuals and families reduce their waste footprint through practical strategies and community support.

Program Components:

  • Weekly Workshops: Learn composting, recycling best practices, and plastic alternatives
  • 30-Day Challenge: Structured program to reduce household waste by 50%
  • Community Swap Events: Exchange reusable items instead of buying new
  • Plastic-Free Pledge: Commit to eliminating single-use plastics

Results: Participants report an average 45% reduction in household waste within the first month.

Green Schools Program

Empowering the next generation with environmental knowledge and practical sustainability skills.

We partner with primary and secondary schools across Cape Town to integrate environmental education into the curriculum through engaging workshops, hands-on projects, and ongoing support.

What Students Learn:

  • Climate Science: Understanding climate change causes, effects, and solutions
  • Biodiversity: Local ecosystems and species conservation
  • Waste Management: Recycling, composting, and circular economy principles
  • Water Conservation: Cape Town's water challenges and conservation techniques

School Gardens:

We help schools establish vegetable gardens where students learn about food security, nutrition, and sustainable agriculture while growing fresh produce for school meals.

Impact:

  • 15 schools participating
  • 2,000+ students reached
  • 8 school gardens established
  • 95% teacher satisfaction rate
